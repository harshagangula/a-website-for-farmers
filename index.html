Seasons in India

Every crop has its own distinct processes of planting, harvesting, and processing. But, broadly speaking, each crop can be differentiated according to cropping season. And in India, there are three main cropping seasons: 1.Kharif
2.Rabi
3.Zaid

Kharif season

The cropping season in India starts in June and ends in October where monsoon crops are cultivated and harvested.
Some important Kharif Crops are:
>>Bajra
>>Jowar
>>Maize (corn)
>>Millet
>>Rice (paddy and deepwater rice)
>>Soybean

Rabi season

The season where crops are sown in mid-November and harvested in April/May is called Rabi Season.
Some important Rabi Crops are:
>>Barley
>>Gram
>>Rapeseed
>>Mustard
>>Oat
>>Wheat
>>Bajra

Zaid season

Since the Zaid crops are also called summer crops, they are sown and harvested between March and June.
Some important Zaid Crops are:
>>Pumpkin
>>Cucumber
>>Bitter Gourd.
